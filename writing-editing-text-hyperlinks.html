Writing, editing text / Hyperlinks

Seb Jensen

I am beginning the production phase of my reimagination of an existing product, specifically my reworking of the Rocket League Esports webpage. I have decided to do so with writing copy and editing the text on the source/original site. In doing so, I have also made use of hyperlinks to incorporate external, official source pages.

Text is essential to conveying meaning and purpose and the inclusion of hyperlinks serves to be beneficial. This will allow me to embed and draw in external information from sources not affected by the kiosk slideshow's offline-only operation.

Writing, editing text

I have collected copy from the source (the Rocket League Esports webpage) and adapted it for my response to the brief. In doing so, I have edited the font - changing the colour, adding bold elements and considered weighting, as well as add italic elements, too. This also includes hyperlinking words/phrases.

Consequently, the text featured in my final kiosk product will be more engaging and fit the design and layout more seamlessly. By cutting out unnecessary details, or alternatively adding more text where it is necessary, I have considered the user experience. This is designed to be a kiosk to be shown in an exhibition or showcase, and all users should be treated as new.

Hyperlinks

I have used hyperlinks to bring in extra information and links to official, external resources from the original Rocket League Esports website. These links will be able to be opened in a browser from within the kiosk, and as such, can be used to provide access to other resources and information that can be kept up-to-date beyond the kiosk.

I can use the 'Action Settings' interface within PowerPoint to easily link up to external resources when an object is clicked. In this case, I have been able to highlight the text and apply the hyperlink to the Rocket League Twitch channel.

Perspective project approach

Strengths included my use of hyperlinks to include access to external, official resources that will not be affected by the kiosk slideshow's offline operation. By linking to the Twitch channel, for example, users will be able to view the livestream.

It would be even better if I hyperlinked more of the text I have wrote and edited for my interactive kiosk. The same overarching reason for this persists - the kiosk will not be able to download and present data from the internet. Hyperlinks offer a means of showing accurate information from official sources.

My short-term goal, specifically for the next session, is to collect the sound, videos, and images that I will need for when I bring all of the elements together for my kiosk product. I intend to organise these in a sensical file structure, where all the files I will need for my product can easily be located with clear names.

My long-term focus remains on how I intend to bring all the elements together. This also includes what assets I want to and/or will need to create myself using production techniques I have previously experimented with. Eventually, I will also need to consider hierarchy can be harnessed to guide the user experience.
