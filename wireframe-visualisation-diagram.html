Wireframe / Visualisation diagram

Seb Jensen

I have conducted wider and focused initial research and identified key features, means of interactivity, sorts of assets I would need. I have also set out and determined the structure and layout of my reimagination of the Rocket League Esports website, in response to the brief while adapting it for a different audience.

I can now consolidate the creative direction I wish to follow and build upon my initial mind map and mood boards. Wireframes and visualisation diagrams are an adequate means of presenting how I wish my product to be designed and set out. In creating the following documents, I am satisfied that I have concluded the pre-production process.

Wireframe

Wireframes aid the pre-production process considerably, in that they allow you to create a quick sketch of where various elements will be placed and interact with one another. They are not time consuming to create as they are very basic diagrams, making them an efficient and effective pre-production document to produce and have on-hand during work towards the production and post-production milestones.

In the above I have laid out two high-traffic slides that will be implemented as part of my kiosk and reimagination of the Rocket League Esports website. I used boxes with crosses to denote images, and lines to denote text/copy. I have also included buttons and other icons and annotations to the side of the wireframe to elaborate on features.

It was particularly quick to produce these two wireframes, thus proving their efficacy in terms of an efficient pre-production workflow and cycle. I was able to include the key details surrounding layout and features with ease.

Visualisation diagram

Visualisation diagrams as a pre-production document could be considered an evolution upon a wireframe. It still demonstrates the desired layout and design, and includes more detail, of the specific design elements I'm wanting to use. They are a lot closer to how the final product will look, but are still relatively quick to produce.

I have taken the two wireframes I already produced and have progressed them into a visualisation diagram.

No annotations

Perspective project approach

I have clearly demonstrated my strengths in the creation of my last two pre-production documents, two wireframes and visualisation diagrams. I have consolidated my creative direction that I set out initially with my mind map and mood boards and developed with my focused research and consideration of structure and layout.

It would have been even better if I created more wireframes and visualisation diagrams to aid my efforts towards the production and post-production milestones. That said, though, it is important to consider the significance of time management when responding to a brief, and I believe I achieved a substantial amount with the sessions I dedicated to pre-production.

My goals now shift to the production and post-production milestones. In the short-term I will have to consider how I intend to progress through the production cycle. I intend to begin with writing and incorporating text and copy into the kiosk, and use hyperlinks to connect slides and external links. As I work through this, I will also need to continually think ahead into the long-term and consider how I intend to piece together all of these different elements.
