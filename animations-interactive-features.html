Animations / Interactive features

Seb Jensen

The use of animations and interactive features will go a long way to enhance the user experience. They engage the user and keep them interested in the content that will be delivered to them by my interactive kiosk product. I identified a number of interactive features that I wish to recreate in my initial research document as part of the pre-production process for my project.

I donâ€™t think I want to include too many animations, but I will be able to use the functionality within PowerPoint to create a gallery as well as attract users to a start button on the splash screen.

Animations

I have used the animations pane functionality included in PowerPoint to create a looping growing animation on the start button. I have used the same asset that I created a couple of sessions ago. This will attract users to the start button, having them press it to begin the experience with the trailer for RLCS that I also downloaded when collecting assets from the source.

Interactive features

Videos

I wish to include the trailer for RLCS that I downloaded while collecting various assets from the source. To remove the unnecessary details at the end of the trailer and therefore make it more appropriate for my new audience, I have used the trim functionality included in PowerPoint to remove the end portion of the video. The slide should automatically advance at the same time the video ends. The inclusion of a video will benefit users who have limited knowledge of the Rocket League Championship Series and Rocket League Esports in general. It will also capture their attention and generate interest in the product, encouraging them to check out the various pages to learn more.

Buttons

I considered the inclusion of various buttons with the creation of my own assets. I have always intended to incorporate a splash screen with a start button, and have animated it as detailed above. Buttons form the basis of kiosk navigation, as the device should be accessible with touch and by using a mouse.

I intend to include buttons on most pages. These will be hyperlinked to other slides in the interactive kiosk, or link to external sources that are not affected by the offline operation of the kiosk. By including information from official websites, I can still offer up-to-date information and resources, such as the live Twitch stream as PowerPoint does not natively support the embedding of them.

The design of buttons, which I have already considered, have a large impact on establishing hierarchy and orientating the user. Some may be larger and more prominent to suggest significance, while others are much smaller and offer less relevant information or lead to slides that are predicted to receive less traffic.

Gallery

For those who are entirely new to the game, let alone any sorts of Rocket League Esports such as RLCS, I am including a gallery of in-game screenshots to help convey the gist of the game visually. As opposed to offering an entire additional slide full of written content about how the game works, by presenting these users with images instead, they are able to form the concept of the game much more easily and naturally.

I am using PowerPointâ€™s trigger animation functionality to show and hide larger versions of a screenshot when their respective thumbnails are clicked. Then, when you click the thumbnail again, the large screenshot will hide. This means that the user can select which screenshot they wish to view a larger version of. I have used many of the original assets that I collected at the beginning of the production process in creating this gallery system.

I can incorporate this gallery into its own slide that will be targeted at users who need to get a better understanding of Rocket League itself. In turn, this will make for a more accessible experience.
